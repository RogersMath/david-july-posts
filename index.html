30 days of ready-to-post content — July 22 to August 20, 2026. Tap Copy, paste into LinkedIn, post. Hashtags included. Personal profile, not the company page.

The strategy (read once, then just post)

This library is built around what actually earns reach on LinkedIn in mid-2026:

  • Post from your personal profile. Personal profiles reach several times more people than company pages — LinkedIn is a people network first, and it pushes human voices, opinions, and lived experience.
  • The first two lines decide everything. Readers see ~3 lines before "…see more." Every post here opens with a hook that earns the click, because reading time (dwell time) is the strongest ranking signal there is.
  • Value beats volume. The algorithm now reads posts for substance and rewards saves, shares, and real comments — while passive likes count for little and engagement-bait ("comment YES for the guide") gets penalized. These posts teach, observe, or ask something real.
  • Stay on one theme. LinkedIn needs to learn what you should be known for. Every post here is wound care — supply economics, dressing changes, long-term care realities. Thirty days of topic consistency trains the algorithm to show you to wound care people.
  • No links in the post body. Outbound links suppress reach. When a post references the NuMed site, put the link in your first comment instead.
  • The first hour matters. Post between 7:30–9:00 AM on weekdays, then reply to every comment within the hour if you can — early conversation extends distribution, and your replies count double.
  • Cadence: one post per day is provided. If daily feels like too much, post the weekday ones and skip weekends — consistency beats intensity. Never post twice in one day.

Personalize freely. Swap in your own phrasing, a real moment from your week, or a photo from a facility visit (with permission). Small personal touches outperform polished copy. Nothing here makes clinical claims beyond NuMed's product labeling — keep it that way when editing.
