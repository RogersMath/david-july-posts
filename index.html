David's LinkedIn Playbook

30 days of ready-to-post content — July 22 to August 20, 2026. Tap Copy, paste into LinkedIn, post. Hashtags included. Personal profile, not the company page.

The strategy (read once, then just post)

  • Post from your personal profile. Personal profiles tend to reach more people than company pages — LinkedIn generally favors individual voices over branded accounts.
  • The first two lines decide everything. Readers see roughly three lines before "…see more." Every post here opens with something worth clicking into, because how long someone actually reads is one of the signals the feed is understood to weigh most heavily.
  • Substance over volume. Saves, shares, and real comments are generally believed to carry more weight than passive likes, and obvious engagement-bait ("comment YES for the guide") tends to get suppressed. These posts teach, observe, or ask something real — nothing here asks for a fake comment to unlock anything.
  • Stay on one theme. Every post here sits inside wound care — supply economics, reimbursement, dressing performance, the realities of running a program. Consistency over 30 days is what trains your network to know what you're known for.
  • No links in the post body. Posts with outbound links tend to get less reach. When a post references the NuMed site, the link goes in the first comment instead.
  • The first hour matters. Post between 7:30–9:00 AM on weekdays, then reply to comments as they come in — early conversation is generally believed to extend how long a post keeps circulating.
  • Cadence: one post per day is provided. If daily feels like too much, run the weekday posts and skip weekends — consistency beats intensity. Never post twice in one day. Dates are checked against the real 2026 calendar, and all four product posts (Jul 28, Aug 4, Aug 11, Aug 18) land on Tuesdays, so the weekday-only fallback still gets your strongest sales content in front of people.

Before you post — two things need your real numbers. The Aug 2 post has a bracketed [X] years placeholder for NuMed's actual combined tenure in healthcare/manufacturing — drop in the real figure. Several posts (Jul 28, Aug 4, Aug 18) reference PDAC-verified coding and free sample shipping without a specific turnaround time, since I didn't want to promise a number that might not match current fulfillment reality — add a timeframe back in if you can commit to one. Nothing else in this batch states a statistic, price, or code that isn't either general industry mechanics or something you can back with your own materials.
